# Pipeline — B2B2C outreach kit

_Internal. Local only — do NOT commit to the public repo._

The goal of this first round is **validation, not revenue**: get 2–3 real conversations,
learn what institutions actually need and would pay for, and (ideally) land one free pilot.
You already have a working demo — the free app — so you can start today.

---

## 1) One-pager (the pitch)

**Pipeline for career services**
*Help every job seeker run a tighter search — without anyone touching their inbox.*

**The problem.** Job seekers lose track of dozens of applications across email; your coaches
fly blind on who's stalling; spreadsheets are manual and always stale. Placement rate is your
KPI, and you can't improve what you can't see.

**Pipeline.** A desktop app that turns each person's application emails into a live,
color-coded pipeline — grouped by company, classified automatically, **running entirely on
their own device**. Career staff get an **opt-in, aggregate** view (who's stalled, response
rates, stage breakdown) — **never the student's actual inbox or messages**.

**Why it fits you.**
- **Better outcomes** — coaches spot stalled seekers early and intervene.
- **Privacy-safe / compliance-friendly** — staff never see private email; only opt-in summaries. Easy story for FERPA/IT.
- **Low lift** — students install and connect their own mailbox; no IT integration.
- **Modern** — a real tool, not another spreadsheet template.

**Pricing.** Per active seat, volume-tiered. **First cohort is a free pilot.**

**CTA.** "Can I set your next cohort up with a free pilot?"

---

## 2) Target segments (best first → why)

1. **Coding / tech bootcamps** — placement rate *is* their product and marketing claim; small enough that the career-services lead is reachable and can say yes. *Start here.*
2. **Outplacement / career-transition firms** (the vendors employers hire during layoffs — e.g. LHH, Randstad RiseSmart, INTOO, Careerminds) — **employer-funded**, high willingness to pay, many users at once. *Highest $ per deal.*
3. **University & college career centers** — real budgets, cohorts, and placement stats feed rankings. Longer sales cycle, bigger seats.
4. **Workforce-development / nonprofit & veteran job programs** — grant-funded, mission-aligned, often eager for modern tools.
5. **Solo career coaches** — many, small; better served later via a self-serve Teams tier.

**Pick 2 to start: a bootcamp + an outplacement firm.** Different enough to learn a lot;
both have a payer who isn't the job seeker and a clear KPI (placement).

## 4) Pilot offer (keep it concrete)
- **Free** for **one cohort**, **6–8 weeks**.
- You provide: setup help, a 20-min onboarding, and direct support.
- They provide: feedback, and — if it helps — a **testimonial / mini case study** and an
  **intro to 2 peer institutions**.
- **Success metric to agree up front** (pick one): % of seekers actively tracking, coach
  time saved, or stalled-application interventions made.

---

## 5) Discovery questions (this is the real payoff of round one)
Ask these on every call — the answers tell you what to build (#1) and how to price.
1. How do your job seekers track applications today? Where does it break?
2. How do you measure placement, and how visible is it *during* the search?
3. Would opt-in, aggregate "who's stalling" visibility (no inbox access) actually help coaches? What would they do with it?
4. What would make this worth a per-seat budget? **What's your budget cycle / who signs off?**
5. What's a dealbreaker — privacy/IT review, integrations, reporting, anything?
